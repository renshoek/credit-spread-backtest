Parameter guide & methodology
Short OTM % — How far below SPY entry the short put strike is. 5% = sell put at 95% of entry price. Lower % = more premium but more frequent losses.
Long OTM % — How far below SPY entry the long put strike is. Must exceed Short OTM. Spread width = Long − Short. Wider = higher max loss but also more premium collected.
DTE — Days to expiration used for Black-Scholes pricing. 30 = standard monthly. 45 = Tastytrade style. Affects only the theoretical premium; settlement is always on the 3rd Friday.
Entry & settlement (Real mode) — Entry price = 3rd Friday close of the prior month (when you'd open the new cycle). Settlement = 3rd Friday close of the current month (actual SPY options expiry). Both from daily SPY.csv. This is more accurate than using month-end closes.
Margin use % — Fraction of capital deployed as margin each month. 25% = professional standard. At 25%, a full-loss month costs −25% of capital. Never go above 30–35%.
Short/Long skew × (Simulated mode only) — Static IV multipliers applied to VIX. 1.30/1.50 are estimates for 5%/8% OTM puts. In Real mode these are replaced by dynamic SKEW-derived values per month.
CBOE SKEW Index (Real mode) — Measures tail-risk pricing. mult = 1 + (SKEW−100) × OTM% / 500. SKEW=130 at 5% OTM → ×1.30. Steepens in crashes, flattens in calm markets. Dynamic per month.
VIX floor / ceiling — Skip months outside this range. Floor: avoid tiny-premium low-vol months. Ceiling: avoid extreme fear regimes. 0 / 999 = always trade.
Profit taker % — Close early when unrealized profit = N% of max profit (premium collected). 50% is the Tastytrade standard. 0 = disabled, hold to expiry. Only applied to clean wins where SPY expires above short strike — for those trades, the spread always decays through the target. Note: taking profit early reduces CAGR vs holding to expiry in most market conditions.
SMA filter — Skip any month where SPY's entry price is below its N-day simple moving average. Designed to avoid trading in sustained downtrends (e.g. 2022). Uses adjusted closes. 200-day is standard; 50-day is more reactive. Note: this is a lagging filter — it protects against the next month, not a crash that starts after you enter.
Stop-loss — Three modes: None = always hold to expiry. × credit = close when unrealized loss hits N× premium collected (Tastytrade standard: 2×). % of margin = close when loss exceeds N% of margin deployed (e.g. 50%). $ per contract = close when loss exceeds a fixed dollar amount (100 shares per contract). With real SPY data, stop is triggered by actual intra-month daily lows, not estimates.
Slippage $ — Per-spread cost deducted from premium (bid-ask + commissions). $1.50–3.00 for SPY. Default $1.50 is conservative.
Risk-free rate — Historical = actual Fed Funds Rate monthly average (FRED). Matters for Black-Scholes pricing.
Win rate definition — Expired worthless: S1 ≥ K1 (full premium retained). NOT dollarPnl ≥ 0. Months where SPY dips slightly below K1 but the loss is less than premium collected are classified as "partial" and counted as losses. This matches industry convention and avoids win rate inflation from small-breach months.
Capital floor — If capital reaches 0, trading stops. No negative equity. A hard ruin event is displayed on the equity chart and in stats.

⚠ THIS IS A SIMULATION, NOT A REAL BACKTEST. Option prices are computed via Black-Scholes using VIX/SKEW data as IV proxy. Even with the corrections below, premiums may still be 10–20% off from actual traded prices. The only fix is real options chain data: OptionsDX (~$100/yr) or CBOE DataShop ($10k+/yr).

ⓘ BIAS CORRECTIONS APPLIED IN THIS VERSION: (1) Convex vol surface — skew multiplier uses a quadratic formula instead of linear, so deeper OTM puts cost disproportionately more in high-SKEW environments. This meaningfully reduces net credit when SKEW is elevated (135+). (2) Fat-tail pricing — put prices use a jump-diffusion mixture model (4% weight on a 4× vol jump component), calibrated to SPY's historical crash frequency. The long put (protection leg) benefits more from this than the short, reducing net credit by 10–25%. (3) Realistic slippage — default $5/contract (was $1.50). (4) Win rate shown as "expired worthless" (S1 ≥ K1), not just profitable months.

⚠ REMAINING BIASES (not fixable without real chain data): No vol-of-vol (VIX can spike intra-month; entry pricing uses prior month-end VIX). No gamma risk near expiry. No dividends (~1.3%/yr). No term structure. Static jump parameters (real crash severity varies). No early assignment. Win rate realistic range: 75–82% for SPY 5% OTM over 2000–2024.
