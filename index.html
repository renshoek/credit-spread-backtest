Parameter guide & methodology
Short OTM % — How far below current SPY the short put strike is. 5% = sell put at 95% of SPY price. Lower = more premium, higher chance of being tested.
Long OTM % — How far below current SPY the long put strike is. Must be deeper than short. Defines max loss width (spread width = long - short).
DTE — Days to expiration. Standard monthly = 30, Tastytrade = 45. More DTE = more premium but more time risk. Note: outcome is still checked at month-end close.
Margin use % — Fraction of total capital deployed as margin each month. 25% = professional standard. At 25%, a full-loss month costs -25% of capital. Never go above 30–35%.
Short/Long skew × — IV multipliers applied to VIX. OTM puts trade at higher IV than ATM (the "skew"). 1.30/1.50 are estimates for 5%/8% OTM SPY puts. Higher = bigger premiums collected. Real skew varies by market regime.
VIX floor / ceiling — Only enter trades when VIX is within this range. Floor: skip low-vol months (tiny premiums). Ceiling: skip extreme fear (too risky). Set 0 / 999 to always trade.
Stop-loss × prem — Close early if loss reaches this multiple of premium collected. Tastytrade standard: 2×. Set 0 to hold to expiry. In this model, stop is approximated at month-end.
Slippage $ — Per-spread cost deducted from premium. Covers bid-ask spread + commissions. Real value: $1.50–3.00 for SPY. Default $1.50 is conservative.
Risk-free rate — Used in Black-Scholes pricing. "Historical" uses actual monthly Fed Funds Rate (0.07% in 2014 to 6.5% in 2000). "Fixed" uses your input. Historical is more accurate.
Intra-month risk — Estimates whether SPY likely dipped below your short strike mid-month using VIX-implied vol (Brownian bridge model). When enabled with a stop-loss, catches cases where the stop would have triggered mid-month even though the month-end close recovered. Without stop-loss, month-end close determines outcome (correct for hold-to-expiry). Breach probability shown in tooltips.

⚠ THIS IS A SIMULATION, NOT A REAL BACKTEST. Option prices are computed via Black-Scholes using VIX as an IV proxy with static skew multipliers. Real options have actual traded prices, bid-ask spreads, and IV surfaces that differ significantly. A real backtest requires historical options chain data (CBOE OptionMetrics, ~$10k+/yr; OptionsDX ~$100/yr). Premiums may be 10–30% off from real market prices.

⚠ REMAINING BIASES: Monthly SPY closes only — intra-month path is estimated, not observed. DTE affects premium pricing but outcome is always checked at month-end (not actual expiry date). No dividend impact (~1.3%/yr). No term structure. No gamma risk. No early assignment. Skew multipliers are static — real skew steepens in crashes, flattens in calm. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Tastytrade Research publishes free verified performance for this strategy type.
