⚠ HOW THE MODEL WORKS: Each month you sell a put at Short OTM % below SPY and simultaneously buy a put at Long OTM % below SPY. The long put is your hard cap — you cannot lose more than (spread − premium) × 100 per contract, regardless of how far SPY falls. Number of contracts = floor(capital / margin per contract), minimum 1 — so recovery is always possible. Capital has no floor and can go negative.

⚠ LIMITATIONS: Black-Scholes underestimates real premiums by 15–30% (IV > RV gap means real income is higher). Excludes IBKR commissions (~€1.30/trade), bid-ask slippage, and Dutch Box 3 tax (36% on gains from 2028). Theoretical model only — always paper-trade before deploying real capital.
